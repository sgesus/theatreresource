Evaluate 6 exams — 2019, 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023, 2025

Definition

To make a reasoned judgement about the quality or effectiveness of something, supported by evidence. Evaluation goes beyond describing or analysing — it requires you to take a position on how well something works and defend that position.

What VCAA has said

  • 2025 "The command term 'evaluate' indicates that the response needs to include an appraisal or judgement of the quality of the work on display. In addition to providing examples and analysing the significance of the choices made, students were required to critique or appraise the final product. In higher-scoring responses, evaluative language was woven throughout the response."
  • 2022 "High-scoring responses tended to demonstrate a strong capacity to evaluate — that is, to explain what was effective and what was not effective in a production. They integrated evaluative language and were able to show a sophisticated understanding of how production roles worked to convey meaning."
  • 2021 "Effective evaluations provided detail of an aspect of the performance that was effective and an aspect that was not effective. Strong evaluations focused on the extent to which a production role successfully worked within specific theatre style(s) to convey meaning, create aesthetically appealing work, or generate feelings for the audience."
  • 2020 "Evaluation is more than analysis — it is about forming a value judgement. Evaluation is not an analysis with evaluative terms shoehorned in; it is not enough to analyse an aspect of the production and insert words such as 'effective' or 'cohesive'."
  • 2020 "Students should be empowered with the capacity to succinctly state their case and to back their argument with detailed evidence from the script and performance."

High-scoring response example

The question — 2025 Exam, Section C Q1b
Evaluate how one or more actors applied your selected aspect(s) of rhythm to establish, maintain and/or manipulate the actor–audience relationship. In your response, refer to one or more specific moments from the performance.

"From the outset of the play, Arundell masterfully harnesses the tempo and pace of her speech; beginning with the lines 'Can you hear me?' Arundell utilises a deliberately slow pace in her speech. This slow pace effectively engenders a sense of calm in the audience and cultivates an intimate actor–audience relationship. However, the restraint of this opening becomes less effective as the play progresses, as the consistent slow pace risks monotony and an eventual disengagement from the audience."

— 2025 Assessor Report, sample high-scoring evaluation

Watch out

"Evaluation is not an analysis with evaluative terms shoehorned in." Simply inserting words like 'effective', 'cohesive' or 'engaging' into a descriptive response is not evaluation. A genuine evaluation makes a clear judgement — effective because..., ineffective because... — and supports it with specific evidence throughout the entire response, not just at the end.

— 2020 Assessor Report

Explain 5 exams — 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023, 2025

Definition

To make something clear by giving reasons. Explaining goes beyond describing what — it addresses how something works and why it is the way it is. The aim is to make the reasoning visible, not just the outcome.

What VCAA has said

  • 2024 "High-scoring responses provided an excellent description of an exercise or task and made highly specific reference to how the element was applied — explaining how this connected to the intended meaning of the script."
  • 2023 "The use of sophisticated, accurate and detailed theatrical terminology was the distinguishing feature in higher scoring responses. A key strategy in preparing for the exam might be developing a glossary of key terms and practising the use of appropriate language to explain work completed in different production roles."
  • 2022 "High-scoring responses provided a thorough explanation of how specific language in the text could inform work and showed a sophisticated understanding of how the context of a university could be conveyed to an audience."
  • 2019 "Low-scoring responses made limited reference to the language of the script or did not explain how their production work could signpost the impending tragedy."

High-scoring response example

The question — 2024 Exam, Section A Q2a
Explain how your selected production role could apply the element of variation in the script excerpt. (Script excerpt from Legally Blonde: The Musical, focusing on Callahan)

"I'd begin this excerpt standing casually yet confidently, shoulders back, one hand in my pocket and the other pointing skywards as I explain. I'd maintain Callahan's confidence and rigidity throughout to show his status and personality, but vary the intensity as the song grows — leaning forward into the space, eyes wide, voice projecting to fill the room — to show his passion for the law and his ability to command the room."

— 2024 Assessor Report, sample high-scoring response

Watch out

"Low-scoring responses failed to explain collaboration with another production role, and some didn't refer to a production aim from the list." Explanation requires the link between your production choice and its intended effect — without this reasoning, a response reads as description only.

— 2019 Assessor Report

Analyse 5 exams — 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023, 2025

Definition

To examine something in detail, explaining the significance and meaning of what you find. Analysis breaks down how something works and why — it requires you to go beyond observation to interpretation.

What VCAA has said

  • 2025 "The command term 'analyse' requires students to explain the significance or meaning of an example. Higher-scoring responses selected one or more strong examples from the performance and used specific theatre terminology when analysing their selected aspect(s)."
  • 2024 "An analysis response discusses in detail how an interpretive choice was made and why it was made. The purpose of an analysis is to unpack the reasoning behind and significance of production decisions."
  • 2023 "Higher scoring responses provided a thorough analysis of work in a production role, including how work in this role could convey shifts in time and place and the significance of those choices for the audience."
  • 2020 "Evaluation is more than analysis — it is about forming a value judgement. It is not enough to analyse an aspect of the production and insert words such as 'effective' or 'cohesive'." [Note: this distinguishes analyse from evaluate — analysis explains how and why; evaluation adds a judgement.]

High-scoring response example

The question — 2021 Exam, Section A Q2a
Analyse how work in your selected production role could convey the Queen's status and her motivation in the script excerpt. In your response, refer to two or more specific lines of dialogue or stage directions and one or more elements of theatre composition.

"To realise the function of the Queen at the moment of her entrance, during the development stage I would use a Pinterest board, collecting images of women in power such as Queen Elizabeth II. I would analyse these women's entrances and how people react to them, and translate this into my costume design by having the Queen's costume use vivid, rich colours compared to the other characters in the scene, which could have dull or muted colours — thus making the Queen the focal point and reinforcing her superior status the moment she enters."

— 2021 Assessor Report, sample high-scoring response

Watch out

"Some low-scoring responses did not analyse an aspect of variation, discuss the intended meaning of the play, and/or refer to a specific moment from the performance." Students often confuse analysis with description — analysis must explain the significance of what you observe, not just state that it happened.

— 2025 Assessor Report

Annotate 3 exams — 2021, 2022, 2023

Definition

To add notes to a text — highlighting, labelling, or marking specific words or lines — in order to show how the language informs your thinking or decisions. Annotations make visible the connection between the written text and your response to it.

What VCAA has said

  • 2021 "The distinguishing feature of effective annotation was a direct connection between the annotated language and how it could inform work in the selected production role. Annotation was managed in a variety of ways, including highlighting, providing footnotes, underlining or circling aspects of text and drawing arrows to an idea."
  • 2023 "The best answers provided a thorough explanation of how three specific moments in the text could inform work in a selected production role — grounding each annotation in precise language from the script."
  • 2022 "High-scoring responses provided a thorough explanation of how specific language in the text could inform work and showed a sophisticated understanding of how the context could be conveyed to an audience."

High-scoring response example

The question — 2021 Exam, Section A Q1a
Annotate Script excerpt 1 on pages 4 and 5 in three places. In your annotations, explain how specific dialogue and/or stage directions could inform work in your selected production role to convey one or more of the five production aims provided.

"[The student highlighted the phrase 'Oh Thomas'] — directly referring to Thomas Kent, I would annotate this with a variety of different reactions of surprise that Viola could be directed to perform on this line, to demonstrate the idea that she is not Thomas, but merely disguised as him. I would explore options ranging from a sharp intake of breath to a sudden stillness — trialling which physical response best conveys the precariousness of Viola's disguise to the audience."

— 2021 Assessor Report, sample high-scoring annotation

Watch out

"Responses that did not score well tended to make general comments about the scene rather than connecting specific language in the text to specific decisions in the production role." Every annotation must be anchored to exact words or stage directions from the script — not to the scene in general.

— 2022 Assessor Report

Identify 2 exams — 2020, 2022

Definition

To name or recognise something specific. Identifying asks you to locate and point to something precisely — it is typically a direct, focused task that may precede deeper explanation or analysis.

What VCAA has said

  • 2023 "Students were required to identify the function and purpose of the character of the Stage Manager, and make reference to conventions from the theatre style, before justifying how their production role could assist in conveying this character." [Identify often leads into further analysis or justification.]
  • 2022 "In one brief sentence, identify this specific moment." [Identify questions are typically short and precise — they do not require extended explanation.]
  • 2020 "This question tested students' ability to identify a character's status and objective — and then to analyse how acting skills were applied to realise them." [Identifying is often the first step before deeper analysis.]

High-scoring response example

The question — 2020 Exam, Section C Q1a
Identify an objective and motivation of your selected character in the play that you have seen in performance.

"Objective: to convince the other characters that he is right. Motivation: a deep-seated fear of being seen as weak or irrelevant, particularly in the presence of those he wishes to impress."

— Illustrative example based on 2020 Assessor Report guidance

Describe 1 exam — 2025

Definition

To give a clear and detailed account of something — what it is, what it looks like, or how it is done. Description focuses on the what and how, without the need for judgement or deep reasoning.

What VCAA has said

  • 2025 "Questions 1c and 1d required students to describe how they would work in their selected production role during the development stage — specifically how they would apply motion and theatre technologies to rehearse, trial or experiment with an idea. High-scoring responses provided specific, concrete detail about the activities undertaken."
  • 2025 "Some high-scoring responses made reference to theatre technologies in the development process, such as the use of digital recording devices to record a rehearsal and reflect on how well ideas are conveyed, or the use of sewing machines to tailor garments."

High-scoring response example

The question — 2025 Exam, Section A Q1c
During the development stage, describe how work in your selected production role could apply motion to rehearse, trial or experiment with an idea to realise the storm scene. In your response, refer to one or more activities used to rehearse, trial or experiment with an idea, and the element of motion.

"To further enhance and refine the scene I can create a set model box in which I can use a laptop to act as a back cyclorama for projections in the model. Incorporating the theatre technology of projections of a storming sky with lightning and big crashing waves that look like they are engulfing the ship, I can experiment with different scales and intensities of the projection to find what best creates a feeling of chaos and confusion."

— 2025 Assessor Report, sample high-scoring response

Justify 1 exam — 2023

Definition

To give reasons that support a decision or claim. Justifying means showing why something is valid or appropriate — not simply stating what you think, but making a reasoned case for it.

What VCAA has said

  • 2023 "Students were required to justify how work in a selected production role could assist in conveying the character of the Stage Manager. They needed to identify the function and purpose of the character of the Stage Manager, and make reference to conventions from the theatre style. Higher-scoring responses provided a thorough justification that connected production role decisions directly to the character's function."
  • 2020 "Students are encouraged to have their own opinion about performances — and to be empowered with the capacity to succinctly state their case and to back their argument with detailed evidence from the script and performance."

High-scoring response example

The question — 2023 Exam, Section A Q2b
Justify how work in your selected production role could assist in conveying the character of the Stage Manager in the script excerpt. In your response, refer to the function and purpose of the Stage Manager and conventions of the theatre style.

"A convention of metatheatre is that there is always one character who plays the narrator, and who directly addresses the audience, breaking the fourth wall. In the play 'Our Town', this chosen character is the Stage Manager. To successfully apply the convention, I would get the actor playing the Stage Manager to step forward and deliver lines directly to the audience, justifying this through the stage direction 'The Stage Manager addresses the audience directly throughout.' This choice reinforces the Stage Manager's function as an omniscient guide — the one character who exists both inside and outside the world of the play."

— 2023 Assessor Report, sample high-scoring response

Show 1 exam — 2022

Definition

To make something visible or evident through specific detail. 'Show' asks you to demonstrate rather than assert — the evidence should do the work, not the claim alone.

Note on usage

'Show' appeared in the 2022 exam as part of an annotate instruction: "Annotate Script excerpt 1 in three places to show how work in your selected production role could apply the selected conventions to interpret the script excerpt." Here it tells you the purpose of your annotation — your annotations must make something visible and clear, not simply note that something exists.

What VCAA has said

  • 2022 "High-scoring responses were able to show a sophisticated understanding of how production roles worked to convey meaning, create aesthetic experiences, and generate emotional responses in the audience."
  • 2020 "High-scoring responses directly quoted dialogue or stage directions from the script excerpt to show the precise language that inspired the interpretation — the evidence itself demonstrated the connection."

High-scoring response example

The question — 2022 Exam, Section A Q1b
Annotate Script excerpt 1 in three places to show how work in your selected production role could apply the selected conventions to interpret the script excerpt.

"[Highlighted 'Darling Journal'] — As the writing in her 'Darling Journal' is the most prominent source of movement for the Princess, I could attach lace ruffs to her cuffs to heighten the languid scrawling pace of this for the audience — showing the contrast between the Princess's slow, deliberate movement and the frantic energy of the dancing couples around her."

— 2022 Assessor Report, sample high-scoring annotation
