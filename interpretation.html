Spelling
How do we write it?

in  ·  ter  ·  pre  ·  ta  ·  tion
Cover it up. Write it from memory. Then check.
Common errors: "interpration" (dropping the e in -pre-), "interpretaion" (missing the t before -ion), "interpetation" (dropping the r). A useful anchor: spell interpret first, then add ation.
Etymology
Where does the word come from?
Origin
Latin — interpretatio, via Old French
From classical Latin interpretatio (explanation, translation), from interpretari (to explain, to expound). Entered English in the 14th century.
inter-
"Between"
A Latin prefix meaning "between" or "among." You can see it in intervene (come between), interact (act between), interrupt (break between).
interpres
An agent or go-between
The Latin root word for an agent who explains one thing to another person — a broker, a negotiator, a translator. An interpres stood between a source and an audience, making meaning accessible.
interpres (a go-between) → interpretatio = the act of standing between a text and an audience, making meaning from it
Definition
What does it mean?
General meaning
interpretation
noun
The particular way someone understands or explains something — the meaning they make from it. An interpretation is one of many possible readings of the same thing; it is not the only answer, but it must be justified.
In Theatre Studies
The creative decisions made by a director, actor, or designer that give a production its distinctive meaning. To interpret a script is to make deliberate choices about what the text means and how those meanings should be communicated to an audience. Every production of the same play offers a different interpretation.
Key idea There is no single correct interpretation of a script — but every interpretation must be justified by evidence in the text. Saying what you chose is not enough; you must explain why.
